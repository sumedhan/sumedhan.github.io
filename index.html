HR to Web Development

How I became a developer

After spending five years defining the HR agenda for unionized workforce in factories and warehouses, I knew I wanted a change. I craved solving analytical problems and learning something new. I had learnt C++ in my undergraduate Mechanical engineering classes and I wanted to explore other technologies. I started with some beginner workshops and online courses and I knew this was the career path I wanted to follow.

A few months later, I quit my job and signed up for Lighthouse Labs' immersive web development program.

My Bootcamp Experience

The Web-dev bootcamp was one of the most intense and exciting 10 weeks of my life. I learnt many different languages and frameworks during my program:

  • Languages: JS, HTML, CSS, Ruby
  • Systems & Databases: Git, PostgreSQL, mongoDB
  • Frameworks & Libraries: Ruby on Rails, jQuery, Bootstrap, ejs, Express.js, ReactJS, Ajax, SASS, knex

But I believe that the most important skill I learnt was to be a life long learner.
